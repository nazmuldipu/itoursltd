Cox's Bazar

Cox’s Bazar is a coastal District of Bangladesh. It is the main tourist spot of the country. More than pne million tourists visit here in a year. It has longest sea beach in the world which stretches 120 kilometres in length unbroken.

Shugandha Beach

Shugandha Beach is the central beach at the Cox’s Bazar city part of the Bay of Bengal. It is cleaner than the other beaches near the city area. Most of the shops, food court & high class hotels are situated around this beach.

Kalatoli Beach

Kalatoli beach comes first of the three beaches close to the Cox;s Bazar city. As soon as visitors reach the city this beach shows its charm to them at first. Most of the local transportation vehicles starts from here.

Laboni Beach

Laboni beach stands at last of the three city-side beaches. It is less crowded than the other two. A very popular market called ‘Burmese Market’ is siuated here. This market is known for things produced by the Burmese people.

Himchari National Park

Himchari National Park is a series of small hills with tropical rain forest. It is situated at 14.1 kilometers south of Cox’s Bazar city. There is a beautiful waterfall along with some small others. Visitors can reach up to one of the hills. From there they can experience an amazing view of sunset at the sea. There is an entry fee of Tk. 30 per person to enter the park.

Inani Beach

Inani beach is a must see while visiting Cox’s Bazar. It is 27.6 kilometers away from Cox’s Bazar city. The main attraction of this beach is that it is filled with coral reefs. Roaming around in the beach in the late afternoons can give a tourist a wonderful experience.

Radar Station

There is a newly built radar station at Cox’s Bazar built by Japan. It is due to forecasting the natural calamities from the sea. It is situated at a hilltop near the beach.

Submarine Cable Landing Station

The Cox's Bazar Cable Landing Station (Cox's Bazar CLS) is currently the sole international submarine cable landing station in Bangladesh, and the Cox's Bazar CLS was built by Bangladesh Submarine Cable Company (BSCCL) for the cable landing of SMW4 in Bangladesh, which lands at the Kalatoli beach in Cox's Bazar.

Light House

The light house is situated at only 2 km away from Kalatoli, Cox’s Bazar. It is better to visit at night as the light of it remains on at night.

Bangabandhu Safari Park

Bangabandhu Safari Park also known as Dulahazra Safari Paark is the largest safari park in the country. It is situated at Chakoria Upazilla, Cox’s Bazar. It is 105 kilometers away from port city Chittagong. It has a huge area of around 2,224 acres (9.00 km2). Dulahazara Safari Park is home to at least 4,000 animals of 165 species. The park itself protects a large number of wild elephants which are native to the area. In the safari park there are domesticated elephants which are available for a ride. Other animal attractions include lions, Bengal tigers, crocodiles, bears, spotted deer and many types of birds and monkeys.

Ramu Buddhist Temple

Ramu Temple is located at Ramu Upazila, in a typical Buddhist village located 16 kilometers away from Cox’s Bazar. It is accessible from the main highway leading to Chittagong. The village accommodates monasteries, khyangs and pagodas. It has images of Buddha in gold, bronze and other metals inlaid with precious stones inside the pagodas.Temple on the Bagh Khali River houses relics along with a 13 feet high bronze statue of Buddha rested on a six feet high pedestal. Wood carvings here is rare and refined. In the village weavers ply their trade in open workshops and craftsmen make handmade cigars.
